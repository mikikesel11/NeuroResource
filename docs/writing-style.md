# NeuroResource — Content & Writing Style

> The voice of NeuroResource is Warm, Direct, and Respectful. We write for
> NeuroDivergent readers first. Clarity is an Accessibility feature.

## The Capitalization Rule (Capitalize Key Terms)

We Capitalize the important **Nouns, Labels, and Concepts** mid-sentence to
give the reader's eye a gentle anchor on What Matters. This is intentional
emphasis, not decoration.

**Capitalize:**

- Core Concepts the page is about — e.g. Focus, Regulation, Executive Function,
  Sensory Load, Burnout, Masking.
- Named Features, Products, and Sections — the Shop, the Resource Library, the
  Adventure, the About section.
- Labels and Categories — Free Resources, Printable Tools, Beginner Friendly.

**Do NOT capitalize:**

- Ordinary connective prose, verbs, and filler. Over-capitalizing kills the
  signal — if Everything Is Emphasized, Nothing Is.
- Words mid-phrase that aren't the concept (e.g. "tools that help with Focus",
  not "Tools That Help With Focus").

**Calibration examples**

- ✅ "Practical Tools for Focus, Regulation, and Rest — built by and for
  NeuroDivergent people."
- ✅ "Browse the Resource Library, or jump straight to Free Printables."
- ❌ "Practical Tools For Focus, Regulation, And Rest." (over-capitalized)
- ❌ "practical tools for focus, regulation, and rest." (no anchors at all)

## Tone & Structure (NeuroDivergent-first writing)

- **Front-load the point.** Say what something is and why it matters in the
  first sentence. No long wind-ups.
- **Short paragraphs, generous whitespace.** One idea per paragraph.
- **Plain language.** Define jargon inline the first time. Offer a TL;DR on
  long pages.
- **Predictable structure.** Headings describe content literally. No clickbait,
  no mystery links — link text says where it goes.
- **No dark patterns, no urgency manipulation.** Especially in the Shop.
- **Literal over figurative.** Idioms and sarcasm are optional cognitive load;
  use sparingly and never load-bearing.

> When this style and an Accessibility requirement ever conflict, Accessibility
> wins. See `docs/system-design.md` → Accessibility.
